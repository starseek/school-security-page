Protecting Private and
Charter Schools

When it comes to private and charter schools, there isn't a one-size-fits-all approach to security. Some schools may rely on the same strategies applicable to public schools, while others may have larger campuses more akin to a university. Regardless of the size of the institution, access control remains a key way to maintain security by restricting both student and visitor access to specific parts of a building or campus. Additionally, camera systems can be used to monitor both interior and exterior properties to give a security manager a full picture of any illicit activities. Hidden cameras can be used to cover blind spots, including mure vulnerable areas like teacher's lounges or offices that house safes. GPS trackers can be used to monitor student transportation, or ensure the efficiency of other school-owned vehicles. By presenting a multifaceted approach to security, managers are able to increase their chances of stopping everything from theft to violent crimes.
